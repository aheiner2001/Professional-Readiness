# **This Week: Resume, LinkedIn, and GitHub**

All three are **required** to present a professional image.  

---

## **Resume Guidelines**

- Use a **template** to make formatting easier.  
- **Name:** Bold, slightly larger than section headers (max 22 pts), preferably **center-justified**.  
- **Section headers:** Bold, slightly larger than normal text (max 2 pts above body text).  
- **Body text & bullets:** Normal font, **≥10 pts** (12 pts preferred).  
- **Contact info & links:** Just below your name; **include active links** to email, LinkedIn, GitHub.  
- **Length:** **One page only**. Avoid shrinking font to fit more.  

### **Content Tips**
- Include **all jobs** you can fit in chronological order.  
- Limit **job descriptions** to 2 lines max, bullets to 1 line.  
- Focus on **important details only**; expand during interviews.  
- Include a **short skills summary** under contact info.  
- List **educational accomplishments and goals** at the bottom.  
- **Be creative with layout:**  
  - Can sections be split into **columns**?  
  - Can a section run **next to another**?  
  - Avoid overcrowding; maintain readability.  

---

## **LinkedIn & GitHub Profiles**

### **Professional Photo**
- Dress in **church/professional clothes** (waist up).  
- **No casual clothing**.  
- Background: clear, light-colored wall (white or near-white).  
- Photo tips:  
  - Portrait orientation, shoulders angled, face forward.  
  - **Smile**.  
  - Take **several photos** with and without flash.  
  - **No other people or pets**.  
  - Crop so **head and shoulders fill frame**.  

### **Profile Content**
- Include **everything from your resume**, even minor or non-technical roles.  
- Highlight **promotions**.  

### **GitHub Specific**
- Contribute to a **project** (yours or others).  
- Options if none exist:  
  - Pick an **existing project** and contribute.  
  - Ask a friend/teammate to **join their project**.  
  - Create a **personal project**.  
  - Upload a **home project** or something you’ve always wanted to do.  

---

**Remember:** Invest the time—it pays off in first impressions.
